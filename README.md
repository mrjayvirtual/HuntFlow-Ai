EHY THIS EXIST ?

I was job hunting across three continents' worth of remote boards — Upwork, We Work Remotely, ZipRecruiter, niche AI boards — with 20+ tabs open, no way to track what I'd applied to, and no honest read on which roles actually fit my skill set versus which ones just sounded good.

So I built the tool I actually needed.
Time to first working version: under 5 minutes, using an AI-assisted build workflow (Claude). No team, no funding round, no six-month roadmap — just a real problem and a fast, iterative build.

That's the whole point: creativity meets technology, and you ship.


WHAT IT SOLVES ?

Job hunting as a self-taught, remote-first creative technologist is messy:

🌍 Eligibility confusion — half the "remote" jobs quietly mean "remote, US only." This tracker flags eligibility per role instead of making you find out after applying.

🎯 No honest fit signal — job boards show you everything; they don't tell you what actually matches your CV. Every role here carries a fit score and a plain-language reason why.

📋 Application chaos — sticky notes, screenshots, half-remembered follow-ups. One board, one source of truth.

🔁 No momentum system — it's easy to apply to 20 jobs in a burst and then go quiet for two weeks. A weekly application goal keeps the habit alive.


WHAT IT DOES TODAY ?

Fit-scored roles across Automation, AI Training/Evaluation, Graphic Design, Growth/Social, Virtual Assistant, and AI-Assisted 

Web Dev — categorized into High / Medium / Stretch probability

Search & filter by tier, category, or keyword

Apply + mark-applied tracking, saved locally so it persists between visits

Per-role notes for follow-up dates, contact names, interview stages

Weekly application goal tracker — built for consistency, not burnout

Add-your-own-lead — extend the board with jobs you find yourself

Copy full list as plain text — because not everything needs to be a download


WHY IS ROUGH ON PURPOSE ?

This shipped in minutes, not months. It's a static, single-file app — no login, no backend, no database yet. 

That's intentional: prove the idea works before investing in infrastructure. The roadmap below is what happens once it's validated by actual use.
